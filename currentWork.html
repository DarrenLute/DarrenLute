Stakeholder Interview

Stakeholder Interview for The Game District

Project Vision

What is your vision for this offering?

I want a place that people can come play and relax. An interface that not only doesn’t get in the way but also helps out the players and GM. I want the software to do what it does best, manipulate the data, worry about the rules; leaving the group to only have to worry about the story.

What defines success for this project?

Initial success for this project would be 100 subscribers and an active marketplace.

What are the potential pitfalls (ie. what keeps you up at night about this project)?

What happens if I can’t garner enough interest?

Users

Different types of users

GM, Player, Artist, Module Designer

Who is primary?

GM

Role(s)?

GM: Set up the games. Determines the parameters and possibly creates a module for the players to play through. Needs access to resources.

Player: Plays the game. Hopefully with the actual rules obfuscated enough to not get in the way, but still obvious about what is going on.

Artist: Creates visual or audio assets that can be used in games. Sold through the marketplace.

Module Designer: Creates adventures that others can use ‘out of the box’. Makes a GM’s life easier.

Value Proposition

What problems do users have that this offering solves?

Location: Allows people to play no matter their physical location.

Time: Allows people to play even if their schedule doesn’t match up with the others in the group.

Rules Overload: Allows the players and GM to focus on story rather than keeping track of the rules.

Interest: Keeps players actively engaged in longer running games. Sends out reminders.

Accessibility: Allows players to access the game in more than just one way (desktop, tables, phones, email)

Persistence: The game is always up so anyone can access it at any time.

Community: A dedicated community.

What is the core value prop of the offering?

Make the game easier. Easier to run, easier to keep track of, easier to set up, etc.

Competition

What similar tools are in use today?

Obsidian Portal - https://www.obsidianportal.com/

Roleplay Online - http://rpol.net/

Roll20 - http://www.roll20.net

Tabletop Connect - http://www.tabletopconnect.com/

3D Virtual Tabletop - http://www.3dvirtualtabletop.com/index.html

Target Market? Value Proposition?

Sites either seem to focus on the player or just give the GM some basic tools to organize their games. I want to provide a platform that not only organizes a GM’s work, but also makes his life much easier.

Your Customers

Target market(s)?

Primary - Play-by-eMail and Play-by-Post.

Secondary - Virtual tabletop.

What role is your team selling to?

Player and GM

User Goals

What defines success? (if an experience goes well, what happens?)

The game flows smoothly without breaking the 4th wall. Games last.

What is a bad results? (If an experience doesn’t go well, what happens?)

The site makes the games harder or more tedious.
